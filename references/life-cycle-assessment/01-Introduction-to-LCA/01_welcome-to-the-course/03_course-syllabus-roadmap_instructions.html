Welcome Message

How many products have you been in contact in the last 24h?

How environmentally friendly is popcorn as a packaging material?

What is best to dry your hands? Paper towels or electric air dryer?

How can I assess the environmental impacts of the 100,000 products on the shelves of my supermarket?

How to compare climate change impacts, human health impact of fine particulate and land use impacts on biodiversity? These questions (among others) will guide our explorations together over the next few weeks.

Welcome to our course “ Life Cycle Assessment - towards sustainable products ”. My name is Olivier Jolliet, and I am passionate about quantifying how much the hundreds of products we are using on a daily basis affect our health and the environment.

To avoid just displacing problems, it is crucial to assess the impacts of the hundreds, even thousands of products we use on a daily basis over their entire life cycle, from cradle to cradle. Life Cycle Assessment will teach you the art of comparing very different products and impacts, how to mitigate them and how to analyze trade-offs in an interconnected world.

I am excited to join you in addressing real-world problems from several industrial sectors (agriculture and foods, automotive, personal care products, and energy) through relevant case studies. in order to demonstrate how effective environmental life-cycle assessment leads to new product development.

Learning Outcomes

After working through this course, learners will be able to:

  • Define the goal and scope of an LCA, in particular the function and functional units as a basis for consistent comparison of products

  • Build the system process tree and define consistent system boundaries

  • Build the Life cycle inventory for establish the primary energy and CO2 balance of a product system

  • Determine life cycle impact, classifying and characterizing impacts at midpoint and damage levels.

  • Interpret results and their robustness, explain study limitations and make recommendations, Identifying the critical points in the life cycle.

  • Perform an LCA case study

Assessments

In this course, there are multiple types of assessments for you to practice your skills and test your understanding.

Quizzes

Our course contains both graded quizzes and ungraded, practice quizzes.

You will encounter the ungraded, practice quiz in Week 1. It is intended to be an opportunity for you to practice constructing a randomized controlled trial, and upon completing the practice quiz you will be provided feedback on your design. As it is a practice opportunity, you may attempt this quiz as many times as you wish, and it will NOT count toward your final course grade.

Modules 1-6 will each conclude with a graded quiz. These quizzes will consist of 5-10 multiple-choice questions. To pass, you must earn a score of at least 80%. You will have an unlimited number of attempts to pass each quiz. These quizzes are graded and will count toward your final course grade.

There is a case study in this course to practice the skills learned during the course. There are two choices for the case study - car alternatives or milk alternatives. Throughout the course, when a new skill or part of the case study is taught, there will be an opportunity to test via activities (ungraded) and quizzes. The first case study quiz is graded. Complete the quiz based on the case study of choice. Before the quizzes, there will be a reflective question to state which quiz you will take. The second quiz will be "dropped" to not count for grades so click through the quiz with the first answers.

Peer Review

There is one peer-review assignment in Week 6: Case Study - Practice Life Cycle Assessment. There will be portions throughout the course that allows practice and preparation for the case study. This assignment requires you to choose one of the suggested products to compare the environmental impacts of two or more product or service alternatives. You will present the results of goal definition and hand calculation of energy and CO2 and account for comments to improve deliverable. After completing your submission, you will then need to review two of your peers’ submissions in order to receive your assignment grade. The grade you receive will be an average of the two scores you receive from your peers.

Grading

The grading policy for this course is designed to capture key learning. Successful completion of all the assignments and tasks within the modules will result in a passing grade.

In order to pass the course, you must successfully complete all of the graded items in the course and you must achieve an overall grade of 80%. The course grade will be a cumulative score based on the course assessments.
