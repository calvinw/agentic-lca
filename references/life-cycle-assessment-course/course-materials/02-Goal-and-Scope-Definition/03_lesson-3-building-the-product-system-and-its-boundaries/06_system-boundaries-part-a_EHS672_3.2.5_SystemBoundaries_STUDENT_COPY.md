- System boundaries: rules for good practice
## System boundaries include all the necessary processes to assure the desired function.
## RULE 1:
## System boundaries should cover the same functional reality in all scenarios (functional boundaries, not geographical boundaries)
## RULE 2:
## Only processes contributing to more than x% of the energy or emissions are retained. The % depends on the level of detail of the study!
## In practice, this cut-off criteria is applied as a minimum inclusion requirement, ensuring that all processes contributing to more than the cut-off are included. All existing processes available in databases or collected data on new processes are usually kept, even if they contribute to less than the cut-off.
## RULE 3:
## Identical processes in different scenarios can only be excluded if the intermediary flows affected by these processes are strictly equal. Still interesting to include these identical processes to identify key production steps and respective shares.

- Summary
## RULE 1 The system boundaries must cover the same functional reality in all scenarios (no geographical or business-based boundaries).
## RULE 2 Cut-off criteria for the inclusion of processes in the system boundary must be clearly described, including assumptions, reasoning and effects on study results. Processes can only be excluded if they contribute less than the cut-off percentages (i.e. 1%) to the mass of the product, the energy consumption of the system, or the environmental impacts (i.e. emissions of a given pollutant) [ISO 14044, 2006]. These cut-off criteria are fixed in advance.
## RULE 3 Processes that are identical in the different scenarios can only be excluded if the intermediary flows affected by these processes are strictly equal (i.e. the same quantity of each process is required per functional unit for the different scenarios).
