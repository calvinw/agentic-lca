- Weighting
- Social values attributed to the different damages (endpoints) considered:
- A) Monetary methods (willingness to pay): transform impacts in costs
- B) Methods involving expert panels or interest groups
- C) Distance to target: Comparison to political objectives
- The way to set the problem is crucial: Much easier to weight a damage than a pollutant quantity or the present level of a given effect; - Always define the absolute value of the damage you are comparing.- Provide panel members with explicit references they can relate to (like total world impact  will weight normalized score – not the raw scores)

- Grouping
- ISO also allows “Grouping” as a qualitative or semi-quantitative process that helps prioritize results by sorting or ranking, and can be done in several ways.
- Impact categories can be grouped according to
- area of protection,
- the types of emissions/resources,
- or by spatial scale (global, regional, or local).
- Impact categories can also be ranked based on a predefined hierarchy; for example, high, medium, and low priority. This second procedure is based on value choices that reflect the importance given by society or the user to a particular category.
