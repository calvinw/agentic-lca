- Original features from IMPACT World+
## Based on IMPACT 2002 which was the first midpoint-endpoint: Midpoint (kgeq) and Endpoint (DALY).

## Major modeling improvements for land use, water and mineral resource use with an extraction-consumption-competition-adaptation approach.
## Acidification and eutrophication with world atmospheric and hydrological fate models.
## Respiratory effects with new intake fractions and epidemiologically derived factors.
## For damage, optional reporting on climate change and water use.

## First signs of maturity as a scientific area, still improving rapidly
## In the field of toxicity, LCIA  is fully part of a collaborative effort with scientists from multimedia model, risk assessment, indoor air pollution and LCIA to defined intake fraction (Bennett et al. 2002).
## Adaptations from risk assessment to the comparative LCIA framework are bringing innovative developments back for comparative risks.
## LCIA are also published in the best environmental journals such as Environmental Science and Technology, Environment international.
## International Journal of LCA, the Journal of Industrial Ecology and the Journal of Cleaner Production are now in the Science Citation.
## 2009: Int J LCA Index 1.7, 60 out of 160 env. journals.
## 2013: Int J LCA Index 2.8, 48 out of 209 env. journals.
## 2019: Int J LCA Index 4.3, 26 out of 350 env. journals

- Summary
- At least include main information on effect (e.g intrinsic toxicity) and fate (tranport, dilution and degradation).
- So far: use several (typically three) impact assessment methods in parallel, e.g. IMPACT World+, ReCiPe, TRACI, IMPACT2002+, or  LC Impact in coming years.
