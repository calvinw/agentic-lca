- Ozone Depletion: Diagnosis BEFORE symptoms
## 1920s: Dobson begins study of stratospheric ozone
## 1974:
## 1985:
## British Antarctic Survey team, which had been measuring the ozone layer over the South Pole for 18 years, discovers the Antarctic ozone hole. This is the first visible evidence that the ozone layer is depleting. The first measurements of loss were documented in the 1980s, but thought it was an instrumental failure, until US satellite confirmed measurements.
## Molina & Roland publish in Nature: CFCs migrate into stratosphere and release Cl.
## 1987:
## (September 16) The Montreal Protocol on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer is signed. It is a legally binding protocol between 24 countries. "Perhaps the single most successful international agreement to date has been the Montreal Protocol."-Kofi Annan, Former Secretary General of the United Nations
## 1989:
## July 1st  All parties to the Protocol freeze production and consumption of CFCs at 1986 levels.
