- Results Interpretation: look from different angles!
## Interpret results from multiple angles, for:
## Each LCA phase: after pollutants inventory,after characterisation and global impact evaluation;
## Each LCA stage (raw material, manufacturing, transportation, use, disposal), concentrating on those which generate the largest impact and on those which offer the best impact reduction potential;
## Each component contribution for a multi-component product.
## Each pollutant and processes, analysing their respective contribution to each midpoint impact category.
## Each midpoint impact category, analysing their respective contribution to each damage or weighted final score per scenario

<!-- Slide 7 -->

- Interpretation question for each scenario
- Which are the largest contribution to the environmental impact in terms of production phase, in terms of pollutant?
## The grouping of processes can be arbitrary and depends on how the system is modeled.
## Also consider large sets of individual processes that each appear to have small impacts but lead to substantial impacts when summed
- Which life cycle stage has the highest potential to reduce impacts (also depending on feasibility)
## In some win-win cases, both impacts and costs can be reduced at the same time.
## Even a limited low-cost intervention can be extremely efficient in reducing impacts.

<!-- Slide 8 -->

## Interpretation questions across scenarios: why? Why? WHY?
## Are the differences between scenarios significant, relevant? For which impact category?
## Are differences/conclusions correct and robust: check check check
## What is the cause for difference in term of process, inventory flow?
## What are the key parameters affecting the environmental impact? (sensitivity studies)
## If some results are surprising: WHY? What are the underlying parameter components and stages responsible for it?
## Never let it go until you understand the rational for a difference or a surprising results. Either you made a mistake … or you will learn something!

<!-- Slide 47 -->

- Expert judgment / default uncertainties
## LCA standardization – default choices
## Expert judgement and default uncertainty estimates
## Minimum difference between two scenarios to be considered as (significantly) different:
## Energy and CO2: at least 10% difference
## Acidification and eutrophication, fine particulates (PM): at least 30% difference
## For toxicity results: at least a factor 10 difference, a factor 100 on individual contribution of substances
## For damage results to compare across impact categories: at least a factor 10 to 100

---

<!-- Slide 48 -->

- Sensitivity analysis and scenario analysis
## Sensitivity analysis: test the robustness of results and their sensitivity to data, assumptions, and models used: vary each parameter ± 10% or better between its high and low 95% confidence limits  look at variation in final impact estimate.
## Uncertainty propagation: Look at the uncertainty on input parameters and how they propagate to the final results using Monte Carlo or Taylor series expansion
## Scenario analysis: study the effect of certain assumptions on the outcome of the assessment. For example, test the importance of the method of allocation by comparing how different substitution assumptions or other allocation methods (eg. financial allocation) affect the final results.
## Empirical evaluation: the only way to test the model validity and uncertainty.

- Product classification depending on dominant LC stage
## MOBILE PRODUCTSA product is mobile if the transport stage is responsible for the dominant energy consumption or impacts.
## Weight reduction is essential for these products. Mobile products can be immobile during use stage but were transported over long distances in other stages.
## ACTIVE PRODUCTS
## A product is considered as active if the use stage is dominant either for energy use or impacts.
## Increasing efficiency or reducing emissions during use is key for improving active products.
## FIXED PRODUCTS
## Products for which the transport stage is not dominant.
## PASSIVE PRODUCTS For passive products the manufacturing and disposal stages dominate impacts.
## Material composition, product effective lifetime and material recycling are key for passive products.
