- Quantitative environmental assessment tools
## LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENTquantifies a large number of resource extractions, emissions of substances to the environment to estimate multiple environmental impacts associated with a given function.
## SUBSTANCE FLOW ANALYSIS (SFA)quantifies the flows and environmental accumulation of either a single substance, such as mercury, or a group of substances, such as inorganic nitrate compounds.
## MATERIAL FLOW ANALYSIS (MFA)tracks the flow of material in the economy of a given region. This is usually a raw material, such as paper, glass, concrete, or plastics.

---

<!-- Slide 6 -->

- Quantitative environmental assessment tools
## RISK ASSESSMENT (RA)studies the risk or the probability of severe impacts occurring from an installation (such as a nuclear power plant) or the risks of using a chemical substance.
## CARBON FOOTPRINT (CF)determines the direct and indirect emissions of greenhouse gases due to a product, a human activity, or a business.
## WATER FOOTPRINT (WF)determines the impacts associated with water as an area of concern, including water usage and environmental exposures related to water quality.
## ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT (EIA) assesses the negative or positive environmental consequences of a new project prior to decision to move forward. It may be governed by rules of administrative procedure
