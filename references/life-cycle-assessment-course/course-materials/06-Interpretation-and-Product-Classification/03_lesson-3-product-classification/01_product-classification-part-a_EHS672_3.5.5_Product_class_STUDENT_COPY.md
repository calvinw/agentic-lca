- Product classification depending on dominant LC stage
## MOBILE PRODUCTSA product is mobile if the transport stage is responsible for the dominant energy consumption or impacts.
## Weight reduction is essential for these products. Mobile products can be immobile during use stage but were transported over long distances in other stages.
## ACTIVE PRODUCTS
## A product is considered as active if the use stage is dominant either for energy use or impacts.
## Increasing efficiency or reducing emissions during use is key for improving active products.
## FIXED PRODUCTS
## Products for which the transport stage is not dominant.
## PASSIVE PRODUCTS For passive products the manufacturing and disposal stages dominate impacts.
## Material composition, product effective lifetime and material recycling are key for passive products.

## Ecodesign strategies – 7R
## Rethink and dematerialize: obtain more service from a similar or reduced materials quantity
## Reduce, especially consumption during use phase of active products + reduce toxic in manufacturing
## Repair or Replace just the obsolete components
## Reuse if material extraction and production phase are substantial  Increase product lifetime and reuse of parts
## Recycle and use recycled materials, if material extraction and emission during end of life or waste treatment are substantial(very valuable and very toxic – trade-off with transport emission)
## Recover energy (cogeneration, incineration, digester)

- Summary
- The dominant stage can guide the optimizationat the early stage of the design
- This classification enables to identify improvement priorities,such as
- Minimum weight for mobile products,
- Maximum efficiency for active products,
- Maximum useful effective lifetime and recycling rate for fixed passive products
- Minimum transport distances for waste disposal
