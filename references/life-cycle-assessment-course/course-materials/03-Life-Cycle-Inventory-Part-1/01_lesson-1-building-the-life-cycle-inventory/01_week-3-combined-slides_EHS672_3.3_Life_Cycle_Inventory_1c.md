- Aggregated or unit processes in databases
- The ecoinvent database provides two types of data”
- the direct elementary flows and intermediary at unit process level (U=UP level), and
- the aggregated elementary flows of a given product, summed up over the entire supply chain from “cradle to gate” (S=system level)
- Many other databases only provide aggregated flows
- Such aggregated inventory flows for different processes can be directly used to build the inventory of a new product that combines these different processes.

---

<!-- Slide 23 -->

- Summary
## The inventory phase of the LCA determines the elementary flows or emissions and extractions per functional unit, accounting for all processes contributing to the project function.
## The inventory is calculated by multiplying the intermediary flow per FU of each unit process by the direct emissions and extractions factors or elementary flows per unit of this same UP.
## It is important to distinguish unit process level inventory data that encompass both intermediary and direct elementary flows, from system level inventory data that have already aggregated elementary flows over the product system supply chain, from cradle to gate.
## Collecting primary inventory data on intermediary and elementary flows is hard work!

- Primary energy definition
## Primary energy is defined as the energy containedin the energy carriers at the point of extraction from the environment, including the upstream energy usage for extraction, preparation, and distribution.
## The final energy is the energy purchased by the consumer and delivered to him.
## The useful energy is the portion of final energy which is actually available after final conversion to the consumer for the respective use.

- Summary
## Input-output LCA (I/O LCA) takes advantage of available economic data to provide economic intermediary flows, without cut-off and also covering services
## I/O LCA is very useful to check process LCA and test whether some important part of the supply chain have been neglected
## However, it is not possible to differentiate product of similar price within a given industrial sector
